Sixth Form

At Sixth form I studied:

  • Computer Science: A
  • Mathematics: A
  • Physics: B
These A-Levels set a perfect foundation for my academic life at Sussex. A-Levels are a challenging qualification to obtain, and because of this I built an excellent work ethic, studying into the late nights and waking up in the early mornings. Putting things in place to make my time easier.

By the end I held an intense love for my studies and research. Hence, award for the Journal Collective mentioned in the highlights section. For context the Journal Collective was a group of students that wanted to take< their studies further in the meantime by researching topics outside the specification of their A-Levels. We also met every week to discuss. My journal entries were mainly focused on electronics, namely my arduino board that I had at home. I wanted to advance my knowledge in mechanical engineering but, I realised there is still alot of electronics involved within mechanical engineering in today.


I also took part in the EPQ. However, I am not as proud of this because, I chose the wrong topic for me and didn't really engage with the topic. My topic was "How effective would artificial intelligence be within the ICU". This paired with that fact that we were in the midst of COVID and I had to switch supervisors resulted in me getting a passing grade.
